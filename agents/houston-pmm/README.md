# houston-pmm

Your AI Product Marketing Manager. Locks positioning, plans launches,
builds battlecards, writes sales one-pagers, analyzes win-loss, designs
messaging tests, and monitors competitors. Never publishes externally
without your approval.

## Who this is for

Early-stage founders doing their own marketing, first PMM hires, and
heads of product who own positioning before a marketing team exists.
Pair with Houston's SDR agent once "hire a team" ships — battlecards
feed the SDR's objection playbook.

## Install

In Houston: **Add from GitHub** → paste this repo URL.

## First prompts

- `Onboard me` — 3-question setup so I can start drafting in your voice.
- `Define our positioning` — Dunford-style positioning capture.
- `Draft a launch brief for {product or feature}` — full GTM brief.
- `Battlecard for {competitor}` — sales-facing competitive doc.
- `What did we learn from Q3 losses?` — win-loss synthesis.
- `Daily standup` — ranked PMM rundown.

## Skills

- **`onboard-me`** — 3-question setup: upcoming launch, competitors,
  voice samples.
- **`define-positioning`** — Dunford-style positioning: alternatives,
  unique attributes, value, best-for market, category.
- **`draft-launch-brief`** — full launch brief with audience, messages,
  channels, asset checklist, success metrics.
- **`create-battlecard`** — per-competitor sales doc: their position,
  our counter, landmines, objection responses, walkaway cues.
- **`write-sales-one-pager`** — segment- or launch-specific sales
  one-pager: positioning, use cases, proof points, FAQs.
- **`draft-launch-content`** — announcement email + LinkedIn long-form
  + Twitter thread variants from the launch brief.
- **`analyze-win-loss`** — extract themes from deal notes / interviews,
  quantify frequency, surface positioning implications.
- **`test-messaging`** — produce 2-3 messaging variants with predicted
  best-fit segment + recommended test design.
- **`monitor-competitor`** — update competitor activity log, flag
  battlecard drift, route sales-relevant moves.
- **`daily-standup`** — morning rundown: launches in flight, stale
  battlecards, competitor moves this week, live tests.
